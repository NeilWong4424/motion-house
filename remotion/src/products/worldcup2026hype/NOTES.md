# worldcup2026hype — product notes

A broadcast hype cut for the 2026 FIFA World Cup, matched to the **official
FWC26 master-brand identity**. Sibling to the `worldcup2026` product (the calm
93s format explainer) — same subject, opposite register, its own design
language and set. 30s, landscape.

## The one message

The scale and inevitability of 2026 — biggest World Cup ever, everything funnels
to one trophy. Feeling, not instruction.

## Official brand — researched, not invented

Brand-researcher findings (2026-07). The counter-intuitive core fact drove the
whole redesign:

- **The master brand is monochrome, NOT the multicolour people remember.**
  Black + white + **metallic gold** (the gold is the trophy render, not a flat
  swatch). Luxury-institutional restraint. The vivid rainbow lives only in the
  16 host-**city** spokes, not the master mark. This film answers the master
  brand.
- **Display type:** the official "FWC 2026" face is a bespoke **ultra-condensed
  black geometric sans**, street-poster character, all-caps in use.
- **Secondary type:** Noto Sans (multilingual). Freely available.
- **Emblem:** a stacked "2"/"6" built from 48 square + quarter-circle modules
  (the 48 nations), a photoreal gold trophy in front, on black. We evoke, never
  reproduce (trademarked).
- **Tone:** "WE ARE 26" — unity across three host nations; hub-and-spoke
  (neutral core + local cultural spokes).

## Design language (design.ts)

- `bg` **#000000** black · `fg` **#F4F4F2** off-white · `accent` **gold**
  (`GOLD` #C6A15B flat; `GOLD_GRAD` amber→champagne for the metallic text).
- Display: **Anton** (substitute — see gaps). Body/labels: **Inter**.
- Accent rule: **GOLD MARKS THE SINGULAR** — the one trophy, the one final, the
  champion. It appears exactly in: the collapse's FINAL slot, the "ONE TROPHY"
  hero, and the closing "2026" lockup + dot. Nowhere else. (The explainer's lime
  sorted teams; this gold points at what there is exactly one of.)

## Gotchas learned (looked at frames, not code)

- **`background-clip:text` goes transparent inside a TRANSFORMED ancestor** in
  headless Chrome. The gold "ONE TROPHY" rendered fully invisible when wrapped in
  `<Breathe>`/`<Rise>`/`<Emphasis>` (all set a transform). Fix: gold text sits on
  a non-transformed div and arrives via **opacity only** (`useEnter`). The gold
  "2026" works because its wrapper doesn't transform the clip node.
- Anton is single-weight and already black/condensed — `H()` ignores fontWeight,
  forces ALL-CAPS, near-zero tracking (condensed faces don't want the negative
  tracking Inter took).

## Honest gap list (flag, don't fake)

1. **Display font is a SUBSTITUTE.** The real FWC 2026 face is proprietary FIFA
   IP with no legal woff2. We use **Anton** to evoke the ultra-condensed heavy
   character — it is NOT the licensed face. To swap the real face: drop its woff2
   in `public/fonts/`, update `design.ts` display spec, re-check wrap-boundary
   text (glyph metrics shift).
2. **Gold hex is undocumented.** No flat brand gold is published (it's a metallic
   render). `GOLD_GRAD` (amber→champagne) is our faithful interpretation; confirm
   against the official Brand Manual Ed.12 if exact fidelity is required.
3. **The emblem/trophy artwork is trademarked** — evoked structurally only, never
   reproduced.
4. **The final's date/venue** ("July 19, 2026 · MetLife Stadium") is unverified —
   confirm before this ships anywhere real.

## Audio (generated music + synth SFX)

**Music is now GENERATED by a third-party AI tool, not synthesized in code.**
Synthesis hit its ceiling (the old `score.py` bed never sounded like a commercial
track). New pipeline:

1. The sound-designer agent writes the music-generation prompt from the locked cut
   → `audio/PROMPT.md` (already produced — the drop is specced to land on 19.6s).
2. **User generates** the track in Suno/Udio/ElevenLabs from that prompt.
3. Agent **aligns** the returned track — `make_audio.py` finds its drop and shifts
   it onto the payoff frame (19.6s, frame 588), pads/trims to cover the full cut,
   writes `<Id>-music.wav`:
   `python make_audio.py WorldCup2026Hype --align <generated.wav> --out out/audio`
4. **SFX stay synthesized** — the same command writes `<Id>-sfx.wav` from
   `score.py compose_sfx` (struck cut impacts, chevron whoosh ~4.55s, gold-logo
   bell stinger 28.47s). Run without `--align` for SFX-only.
5. Mux aligned music + SFX per CLAUDE.md, verify the agent's numeric gates
   (drop within ~0.1s of 19.6s, LRA ≥ 5 LU, True Peak ≤ −1.5 dBTP).

Muxed onto the silent render → `renders/worldcup2026-hype-final.mp4`.

> Note: the old fully-synthesized bed (`score.py compose_music`) is superseded for
> delivery. It remains only as a scratch reference; do not ship it.

- **Register (v3 — produced track, not a bed):** the score is arranged like a
  commercial sports-open, not laid under the picture. A real **drum bus** (128bpm
  four-on-the-floor kick + backbeat snare/clap on 2&4 + 8th/16th hats), a repeating
  **hook** (A4 C5 E5 D5 supersaw shout, answered higher — E5 G5 C6 A5 — at the
  drop), **sidechain pump** ducking the sustained bed to the kick, and a real
  **arrangement**: sparse intro → build → riser (15.0→20.07s) → DROP on the "ONE
  TROPHY" payoff frame → outro. Harmony lifts A-minor→brighter at the drop; kick
  drops out for the hero's air. SFX: struck impacts on every hard cut, whoosh into
  the chevrons (~4.55s), ringing bell stinger on the gold "2026" (28.47s).
- **Mux values:** music `volume=0.32`, sfx `volume=0.70`, `amix normalize=0`.
- **Measured (v3 final mp4):** Integrated **−20.6 LUFS**, True Peak **−1.8 dBTP**
  (3 dB air, not hot), **LRA 6.7 LU** (up from the flat 3.9 — it breathes).
  Intro@1s **−33.6 dB** → drop@19.6s **−20.8 dB** = **+13 dB** contrast (the drop
  lands). Cut@11.0s peaks **−3.8 dB** vs lull@12.5s **−13.5 dB** (SFX on-frame,
  +9.7 dB). Stinger is the loudest moment (−1.8 dB). ✔ all agent gates.
- **Synthesis ceiling (still real):** v3 is a proper produced arrangement, but it
  IS synthesized — a licensed sports-open track would still beat it (a hook played
  by real instruments, a mastered drop). That remains the biggest audio upgrade
  and a budget decision, per CLAUDE.md money-gated deliverables.

## What I'd still improve (ranked)

1. **Licensed music** — the synth bed is good but has a ceiling; a real

2. Confirm the final date/venue (gap #4).
3. The bracket-collapse sits slightly high-left; a touch more vertical centring
