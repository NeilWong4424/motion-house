# Sound design — half the production value

A genre chapter, and the sound-designer agent's bar. Read `motion-craft.md`'s
Audio section first; this deepens it. Product-neutral: exact loudness targets,
instruments, and cue frames are per-film and live in the product's `NOTES.md`.

## The one idea

**Sound is not laid under the picture — the picture is cut to the sound.** Music
defines pacing. Score first, edit to it, and the cuts land on the beat and
disappear. Audio bolted on afterward always sounds bolted on.

## Mix

- **Music is a BED.** Low, with generous headroom, never near clipping. A hot mix
  (loud, peaking) is one of the fastest tells of cheap work — measure the
  reference with `volumedetect`, match its loudness, and record the targets in the
  product's notes. The bed sits far below where an amateur puts it.
- **SFX read ABOVE the bed, briefly.** A UI tap or a whoosh peaks over the music
  for its instant, then is gone. Verify a SFX window still peaks above the bed.
- **Duck under the payoff.** At the film's turn, pull everything back for a moment
  so the hero beat has air. Contrast is what makes the lift land.

## Score

- **Write it to the cut.** Chord changes land on the actual scene-change frames;
  the harmony lifts at the turn. A generic loop under the picture is filler and
  sounds like it.
- **Synthesis has a ceiling.** A four-chord sine pad is filler. If you must
  synthesize: a real progression with voice leading, struck timbres with
  inharmonic partials, an arrangement that builds. Licensed/commissioned music
  beats the best synthesis — if the budget exists, use it (a `craft` decision the
  film should flag, not hide).
- **The accent instrument, used once.** Reserve one timbre (a bell, a hit) for the
  single most important moment — scarcity, exactly like the visual accent.
- **Genre sets the palette.** Calm/editorial: felt piano, bowed strings, no drums
  (a pulse makes it a promo). Broadcast/energy: a real rhythmic pulse IS the point
  — score to a grid and cut to it. Match the score to the design language.

## SFX

- **Land ON the event frame.** A pop three frames late reads as broken. Derive cue
  frames from the composition's actual frame constants, never a stale layout.
- **Design, don't sample-dump.** A send, a receive, a reveal each get a
  purpose-built sound — short, clean, consistent across the film.
- **The score covers the full cut.** Audio ending early is worse than no audio.

## Reference bar

An Apple film's score; a broadcast package's stinger design; a game trailer's mix.
The music carries the pacing and you never notice the mix — which is the point.
