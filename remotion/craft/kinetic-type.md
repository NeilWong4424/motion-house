# Kinetic typography — type as motion

A genre chapter. Read `motion-craft.md` first — this deepens its Type section for
title sequences and big-type films where the words ARE the graphic. Primitives:
`shared/motion/type-kinetic.tsx` (+ `TextReveal` in reveal.tsx).

## The one idea

**Type that moves is still type that must be read.** Every kinetic device buys
attention; it pays it back only if the word is legible after it stops. If the
motion makes the word harder to read, it's decoration, and decoration on type is
the fastest way to look like a template.

## Rules

- **Big, or don't bother.** Kinetic type is display type — 80px+ at 1080p.
  Animating a caption is fidgeting.
- **One device per line, not a parade.** A line can track in, OR swap a word, OR
  get a highlight sweep — pick one. Stacking three kinetic effects on one line is
  a showreel.
- **Per-character motion: 1–3 words only.** `TrackingIn` and character reveals
  read as premium on a title and as noise on a sentence. `LineMask` (per-line) is
  the safe device for multi-line copy.
- **Emphasis is scarcity.** `Emphasis` on one word works because the others hold
  still. Pop every word and nothing is emphasised.
- **The swap is a thought changing.** `WordSwap` ("now" → "today") is a rhetorical
  move — use it where the meaning genuinely shifts, not for motion's sake.
- **Highlight sweeps land on the payoff word.** `HighlightSweep` under the word
  the line turns on; the accent belongs to the design language, used rarely.
- **Hold ≥ 1s per line.** The reader reads after the motion stops. Cutting on the
  settle is the amateur tell.

## Reference bar

Apple's type-forward keynote slides; broadcast title sequences; lyric films done
well. All set type enormous, move it once, and let it sit. None of them animate
every word.
