# Broadcast energy — rhythm, shapes, confident cutting

A genre chapter. Read `motion-craft.md` first — this is its energetic counterpart.
Where the flagship teaches restraint (Apple keynote calm), this teaches the
LoL-Worlds / sports-broadcast register: high energy, confident graphic shapes, cut
to a beat. Primitives: `shared/motion/graphic.tsx`.

## The one idea

**Energy is rhythm, not chaos.** A high-energy package is not "more motion
everywhere" — it's a strong, regular pulse that everything cuts to. The restraint
rule still holds; it just runs faster. Busy is still amateur. The difference from
the calm register is tempo and graphic confidence, not the loss of discipline.

## Rules

- **Cut to the music's beat.** This register is scored first and cut to the score
  — the edit lands on the downbeat and disappears. If cuts don't align to the
  pulse, it reads as frantic, not energetic.
- **Confident graphic shapes.** Bold rules, brackets, badges, chevrons drawing on
  with `LineDraw` — big, fast, decisive. A timid thin line at this tempo looks
  weak; commit to the shape.
- **Faster timing, same curves.** Entrances 8–14 frames (vs 12–20 calm), staggers
  2–3. Still eased — fast-out/slow-in — just compressed. Never linear.
- **Accent works harder here, but is still a system.** Two accents max (a primary
  + a hot secondary). A broadcast look can carry more colour than editorial, but a
  rainbow is still noise.
- **Hold the hero beat.** Even at pace, the key stat / the winner / the headline
  gets a real hold. Energy without a landing is exhausting; the hold is the payoff.
- **Directional continuity.** Shapes and pushes move consistently (left = forward).
  In a fast cut, inconsistent direction reads as jitter.

## Reference bar

LoL Worlds format explainers; NBA/football broadcast opens; esports lower-thirds.
Loud, fast, and still perfectly controlled — every element on the beat.
