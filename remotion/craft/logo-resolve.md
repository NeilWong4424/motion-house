# Logo resolve — how a mark lands

A genre chapter. Read `motion-craft.md` first. Every launch/brand film ends on a
lockup; this is how it should land. Primitives: `shared/motion/logo.tsx`.

## The one idea

**The resolve is the film's last held frame — earn it, then hold it.** The logo
is where the film pays off. It should arrive with weight, settle, and STOP, held
long enough to imprint. A logo that spins, shines, or bounces reads as a free
template; a logo that assembles with restraint reads as a brand.

## Rules

- **Assemble, don't perform.** `MarkDraw` (the mark stroke-reveals) → `Lockup`
  (wordmark settles in) → `Resolve` (the accent lands once). Each step is a quiet
  arrival, not a trick. No 3D spins, no lens flares, no shine sweeps.
- **The accent lands ONCE, last.** A single accent element — a dot, a rule, a
  tagline — arrives after a deliberate beat, and that's the final gesture.
  Scarcity is the whole effect.
- **Weighty timing.** Use the heavier springs (slower arrival) — a logo is the
  hero moment, not a UI chip. It should feel like it has mass.
- **Then HOLD.** The resolve holds 1.5–2.5s of stillness before the film ends.
  Cutting a logo the instant it lands throws away the payoff. This is the single
  most common place films rush.
- **Silence or a single note.** Pair the landing with a clean stinger or a held
  chord — never a busy sting. The audio scarcity mirrors the visual.
- **Respect the mark's construction.** Draw order and proportion follow the real
  logo's geometry (from brand-researcher). Don't invent how the mark builds.

## Reference bar

The close of an Apple product film; a broadcast network ident; a game studio
splash. The mark arrives, the room goes quiet, and it holds.
